Jupiter:

Jupiter is the fifth planet from the Sun and the largest planet in the Solar System. It is a gas giant, meaning it is made mostly of hydrogen and helium rather than having a solid surface like Earth. Jupiter is more than 11 times wider than Earth and has a powerful magnetic field. One of its most famous features is the Great Red Spot, a giant storm that has been raging for hundreds of years.

Jupiter rotates very quickly, completing one rotation in about 10 hours, making it the fastest-spinning planet in the Solar System. However, it takes about 12 Earth years to orbit the Sun once. The planet has a faint ring system and more than 90 known moons, including Ganymede, the largest moon in the Solar System, as well as Europa, Io, and Callisto.

Jupiter is named after the king of the Roman gods. Because of its enormous size and strong gravity, it plays an important role in shaping the Solar System. Scientists study Jupiter and its moons to learn more about the formation of planets and the possibility of life beyond Earth.

The Great Red Spot is a massive storm located in Jupiter's atmosphere. It is so large that Earth could fit inside it more than once. Scientists have observed the storm for over 300 years, making it one of the longest-lasting storms ever discovered in the Solar System. Although it has been shrinking in recent years, it remains one of Jupiter's most recognizable features.
